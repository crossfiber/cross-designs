What a redesign includes.

01

An honest teardown first

Before anything gets rebuilt, you get a straight assessment: what the current site does well, where it leaks leads, and what is worth keeping. Sometimes the answer is a partial fix, and I will say so.

Rankings preserved

Pages that already rank keep their URLs or get properly redirected. A redesign should build on the SEO equity you have, not torch it, which is the classic redesign mistake.

Old template sites drag around plugins and page builder bloat. The rebuild is hand-coded and lean, which customers feel and Google measures.

A design that matches your work today

Businesses outgrow their websites. The redesign catches the site up to the operation you actually run now, with current photos, current services, and current prices.
